### Background

I started preparing to study abroad in my junior year, but my idea of studying abroad basically dates back to when I first entered university. Before the epidemic 20 years ago, my goal was to go abroad for graduate students.

However, since the autumn of 2019, the wind and rain have suddenly changed. Sun Yat-Sen University originally had a UCB exchange program every semester, but the program was announced to be canceled during my sophomore year, and the cancellation time was just after the application deadline for the 20 Spring semester program, which meant that By accident, I became the first student whose program was cancelled.

In addition, with the arrival of the 20-year epidemic, I spent the first half of the entire 20 years at home - until the start of school at the end of August. During this period, I experienced an unprecedented 8 weeks of online classes, during which the process of studying abroad was completely put on hold: the willingness to go abroad began to diminish with the spread of the epidemic and the opposition of my parents, and it was not until November 20 that I took it. The first standardized test: GRE. However, basically empty-handed, I soon ushered in a fiasco.
